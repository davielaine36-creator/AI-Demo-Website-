# Laine Industries — Offer Sheet

Simple, flexible offers. These are **starting points**, not rigid packages — we
recommend the smallest useful version first. Prices are **rough ranges** for
early / pilot builds and are confirmed after a short intake or call. Nothing here
is a guarantee of results.

> Internal note: keep pricing flexible. When unsure, quote the low end as a
> "starting point" and confirm scope before committing. It's fine to offer pilot
> pricing to the first handful of clients.

---

## 1. Automation Audit

- **Who it's for:** Owners who feel busy and disorganized but aren't sure what to
  fix first.
- **Problem it solves:** No clear picture of where leads, follow-ups, and manual
  work are leaking time and money.
- **What's included:**
  - A short intake + one call to understand how the business runs today.
  - A simple written map of where leads and follow-ups are slipping.
  - A prioritized list of 3–5 practical fixes, smallest-useful-first.
  - A clear recommendation for a starter build (with rough pricing).
- **What's not included:** Building anything yet — this is the diagnosis, not the
  build. (The audit fee can be credited toward a build.)
- **Rough starting price:** ~$150–$400 (or offered free/credited for early pilots).
- **Timeline:** 3–5 days.
- **Best next step:** Short intake → 20-minute call → written audit.

---

## 2. Starter Website + Intake System

- **Who it's for:** Businesses with no website, an outdated one, or a site that
  doesn't clearly explain what they do.
- **Problem it solves:** Customers can't quickly understand the business or an
  easy way to reach out, so leads are lost before they start.
- **What's included:**
  - Clean, mobile-friendly website or landing page.
  - Clear services section and simple, obvious calls-to-action.
  - Contact / quote-request form that lands somewhere organized.
  - Basic SEO structure so the business can be found.
- **What's not included:** Ongoing content writing, blog management, paid ads, or
  complex e-commerce (quoted separately).
- **Rough starting price:** ~$500–$2,000 depending on pages and complexity.
- **Timeline:** 1–3 weeks.
- **Best next step:** Short intake → we send 1–2 quick layout ideas.

---

## 3. Lead Capture + CRM Dashboard

- **Who it's for:** Service businesses whose leads and jobs live in texts, calls,
  memory, and scattered notes.
- **Problem it solves:** No single place to see who's a lead, what stage they're
  at, and what needs follow-up — so jobs slip.
- **What's included:**
  - Simple dashboard for names, phone numbers, job details, and notes.
  - Lead/job statuses (e.g. new, contacted, quoted, scheduled, completed).
  - Follow-up fields and reminders.
  - New-lead alerts so you respond faster.
- **What's not included:** Deep integrations with large enterprise CRMs, custom
  accounting, or payroll systems (can be scoped later).
- **Rough starting price:** ~$750–$2,500 depending on fields, statuses, and alerts.
- **Timeline:** 1–3 weeks.
- **Best next step:** Short or full intake → we map your current process first.

---

## 4. AI Follow-Up Drafting System

- **Who it's for:** Owners who lose jobs to slow or inconsistent follow-up, or who
  hate rewriting the same messages.
- **Problem it solves:** Follow-up is slow, inconsistent, or forgotten — and
  writing every message from scratch is a chore.
- **What's included:**
  - AI-drafted follow-up emails/messages based on customer status.
  - Reusable reply templates for common situations.
  - Optional owner approval before anything sends (you stay in control).
  - Reminders for same-day, few-day, and later follow-ups.
- **What's not included:** Fully autonomous sending without review (by design),
  and we don't promise specific reply or conversion rates.
- **Rough starting price:** ~$500–$2,000, often added onto a CRM/dashboard build.
- **Timeline:** 1–2 weeks (faster when paired with a dashboard).
- **Best next step:** Full intake (the follow-up section helps us tailor tone).

---

## 5. Monthly Support / Maintenance

- **Who it's for:** Clients who want their system kept current and improved over
  time without hiring in-house.
- **Problem it solves:** Systems drift out of date; small tweaks pile up; nobody
  owns ongoing improvements.
- **What's included:**
  - Ongoing small tweaks, fixes, and improvements.
  - Priority support and quick questions answered.
  - Light monthly check-in on what's working and what to improve next.
- **What's not included:** Large new builds or major features (scoped as separate
  projects), and hard uptime/SLA guarantees.
- **Rough starting price:** ~$100–$500/month depending on scope and response time.
- **Timeline:** Ongoing, month-to-month, cancel anytime.
- **Best next step:** Add on after a build ships and the system is live.

---

## How to present offers on a call

1. Start with the **Automation Audit** framing even if they buy a build — it
   shows you diagnose before you sell.
2. Recommend **one** starter build (the smallest useful one). Don't stack.
3. Mention **Monthly Support** only after they're excited about the build.
4. Always tie the offer back to their own words ("you mentioned leads slip when
   you're on a job — here's the piece that fixes that").
