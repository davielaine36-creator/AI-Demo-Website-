# Lane Industries — Outreach Kit

Plain-English messages for reaching out to small businesses. Copy, paste, and
tweak the details for each business (use their name, their trade, their city).

**Golden rules**
- Lead with the problem, not the tech. Talk about lost leads and slow follow-up,
  not "AI agents."
- Keep it short. One clear idea per message.
- Always end with one small, easy next step (a quick reply, a link, a call).
- Never invent results, clients, or guarantees. It's fine to say we're early and
  taking on a few pilot builds.
- Point people to the site: `laneindustries.dev` (intake, demos, contact).

---

## One-sentence pitch

We help small businesses turn scattered leads, missed follow-ups, and outdated
websites into one simple system — so nothing falls through the cracks.

---

## 5 plain-English explanations of what Lane Industries does

1. We build simple websites that clearly explain what a business does and make it
   easy for customers to reach out.
2. We set up one organized place (a simple dashboard) for leads, customer info,
   job notes, and follow-ups — instead of texts, memory, and sticky notes.
3. We use AI to draft follow-up emails and messages, so owners can respond fast
   without writing every message from scratch.
4. We set up alerts and reminders so you know the moment a new lead comes in or a
   job needs attention.
5. We help online stores (like Shopify) stay organized — products, customer
   issues, and day-to-day tasks in one tidy system.

## 5 direct outreach messages (local service businesses)

**1. The "lost leads" angle**
> Hi [Name], I came across [Business] and had a quick question — when someone
> fills out a form or texts you for a quote, what happens next? A lot of local
> businesses lose a few jobs a month just because follow-up slips. I build simple
> systems that capture every lead and draft the follow-up for you. Happy to show
> you a quick example if it's useful — no pressure.

**2. The "one place" angle**
> Hi [Name], I help service businesses like [Business] get all their leads,
> customer info, and follow-ups into one simple dashboard — instead of living in
> texts and notebooks. It usually takes the daily chaos down a notch. Would it be
> worth a 10-minute look? I can send a short example first.

**3. The "website that actually explains you" angle**
> Hi [Name], I build clean, simple websites for local businesses that make it
> obvious what you do and easy for customers to request a quote. I looked at
> [Business] and had a couple of quick ideas. Want me to send them over?

**4. The "faster response" angle**
> Hi [Name], quick one — most customers go with whoever replies first. I set up
> simple systems that alert you to new leads and draft the follow-up message
> automatically, so you're not losing jobs to slow replies. Would that be helpful
> for [Business]?

**5. The "pilot build" angle (honest + early)**
> Hi [Name], I run a small studio that builds simple lead + follow-up systems for
> local businesses. We're taking on a few pilot builds right now and I'd love for
> [Business] to be one. If you've ever felt like leads or follow-ups slip through
> the cracks, I can show you a simple fix. Open to a quick chat?
