# Laine Industries — First 10 Clients Plan

A simple, honest plan to land the first 10 clients. The goal early on isn't
volume — it's a handful of real builds you can do exceptionally well and learn
from. Momentum over perfection.

---

## Who to contact first (in order)

1. **Warm circle first.** Friends, family, and anyone you already know who runs a
   business. Lowest friction, most honest feedback, fastest first "yes."
2. **Businesses you have a real connection to.** Brown Academy (training) and the
   window-service / construction contact are natural first pilots — you already
   understand their world.
3. **Local service businesses within reach.** Contractors, window cleaners,
   cleaners, landscapers, detailers, small training/education outfits — trades
   where slow follow-up clearly costs jobs.
4. **Shopify / small ecommerce owners** in your network — for operational cleanup
   and drafted replies.
5. **Referrals** from anyone above, even before a build ships ("know anyone
   drowning in leads?").

> Start where trust already exists. The first 3 clients almost always come from
> people who already know you or a business you understand.

---

## How to use the pilot examples (Brown Academy / window-service)

These are honest, in-progress pilots — use them as **concrete examples of the kind
of system you build**, never as finished case studies with fake results.

- **Say:** "I'm building a lead + follow-up system for a window-service business —
  quote requests come in, they get captured and organized, the owner gets an
  alert, and the follow-up gets drafted automatically. I could set up something
  similar for you."
- **Say:** "One of our pilots is a training company — website cleanup, course
  info, and follow-up reminders so student inquiries don't slip."
- **Don't say:** "We increased their revenue by X%" or anything you can't back up.
- Point people to `laneindustries.dev/case-studies` and note they're pilots in
  progress — being early and honest is a selling point, not a weakness.

## Daily outreach targets

Keep it small and sustainable so it actually happens every day:

- **5–10 new outreach messages** (mix of warm + local businesses).
- **3–5 follow-ups** to people who didn't reply yet.
- **1 personalized message** to a dream/priority business (extra effort).
- **Log every contact** (name, business, channel, date, status) in a simple
  sheet or the dashboard you're building — dogfood your own product.

That's ~15 minutes to an hour a day. Consistency beats big bursts.

---

## Weekly targets

- **30–50 total outreach touches** (new + follow-ups).
- **3–5 real conversations** (call, DM thread, or meeting).
- **1–2 intake forms** completed.
- **1 pilot build started or scoped** once you have interest.
- End each week with a 20-minute review: what's working, what to change.

---

## What counts as progress

Progress is **not** just signed deals early on. Count these as wins:

- A reply that turns into a real conversation.
- A completed intake form.
- A discovery call booked or done.
- A referral or intro.
- A pilot scoped, even unpaid or low-cost.
- A clear "no" with a reason (tells you who to target better).

Track leading indicators (conversations, intakes) — the deals follow.

## How David and Eliseo should split tasks

Play to strengths and avoid stepping on each other. Suggested split:

- **One person owns outreach + relationships** (sending messages, follow-ups,
  discovery calls, keeping the tracker current).
- **One person owns building + delivery** (websites, dashboards, follow-up
  systems, testing, handoff).
- **Shared, weekly:** 30-minute sync to review the pipeline, decide who to
  prioritize, and agree on scope/pricing for anything new.
- **Whoever runs the call** writes a 3-line recap afterward so the other can pick
  up the build with full context.

Swap roles occasionally so both understand the whole business — but keep clear
ownership week to week so nothing is "someone else's job."

## What to do after the first interested lead

1. **Reply fast** — ideally within the hour. You're modeling the exact thing you
   sell (fast follow-up).
2. **Send the short intake** (`/intake`). Keep the first ask tiny.
3. **Book a 15–20 minute discovery call** (use `discovery-call-script.md`).
4. **Recommend one small starter build** tied to their own words — don't stack
   offers.
5. **Send a simple written plan + price** within a day of the call.
6. **Deliver the pilot exceptionally well.** Over-communicate, hand it off
   clearly, and ask for honest feedback.
7. **Ask for a referral and (once there are real results) permission to use it as
   a case study.**

---

## First 90 days — simple milestones

- **Weeks 1–2:** Set up the tracker. Contact the full warm circle + first pilots.
- **Weeks 3–6:** First discovery calls and intakes. Start 1–2 pilot builds.
- **Weeks 7–12:** Ship the first builds, gather honest feedback, convert real
  outcomes into case studies, and lean into referrals.

Goal by day 90: a few shipped builds, real examples to show, and a repeatable
weekly rhythm — not overnight scale.
