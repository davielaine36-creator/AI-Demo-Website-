# Laine Industries — Client Questions & Plain-English Answers

Founder training + call prep. This is how David and Eliseo should *talk* to
small-business clients — calm, honest, jargon-free, and protective of both sides.
Read it, practice it out loud, and use the same language so Laine Industries sounds
like one company.

> The whole game: **translate, don't explain the stack.** The client is buying
> confidence that you understand their problem, won't lose their info, won't
> disappear, and won't make them learn tech. Answer like that.

---

## The mindset (read before every call)

The client isn't buying "a website" or "an automation." They're buying:

> "This person gets my problem, can explain the process, won't lose my
> information, won't vanish, won't make me learn tech, and won't leave me with
> some confusing thing I can't use."

Your job on a call is **calm certainty**:
- I know how this process works.
- I know what I need from you.
- I know what I will and won't promise.
- I know how to protect both of us.
- I'll recommend the cleanest path.

You do **not** need to know everything perfectly. You need to sound like a
responsible professional who collects the facts, checks the best path, and comes
back with a recommendation. That's literally what consultants do.

## The Q&A bank

Short, sayable answers. Adapt to the person in front of you.

### What exactly do you do?
> "We build simple systems that help small businesses capture leads, stay
> organized, and follow up faster — websites, lead trackers, and AI-assisted
> follow-up. We handle the technical side so you don't have to."

### How does this work?
> "We start with a short intake to understand your business. I recommend the
> smallest useful system, give you a clear price, we build and test it, then hand
> it off with a walkthrough. Simple and step by step."

### Where does the website or app live?
> "It's hosted on professional, reliable hosting that we set up and manage for
> you. You get a live link that works — you don't have to touch or understand the
> hosting side."

### Do I need GitHub, Vercel, hosting, or technical accounts?
> "No. That's our world, not yours. We manage all the behind-the-scenes tools.
> You just get the working system and a walkthrough — unless you specifically want
> to own those accounts, and then we can set that up."

### Do I own the system?
> "You own your business information, content, brand assets, and customer
> relationships — always. The technical setup depends on the project: we can
> manage it for you, build inside your own accounts, or transfer it to you later.
> After intake I'll recommend the cleanest option for you."

### Can you manage everything for me?
> "Yes — that's what most clients choose. We build it, host it, keep it running,
> and handle small changes. You get a system that works and someone to call."

### Can I own the accounts instead?
> "Absolutely. We can build inside your own accounts so you own everything from
> day one. It's a little more setup on your side, and the build fee reflects that,
> but some owners prefer it."

### Can it be transferred later?
> "Yes. A lot of people start with us managing it, then transfer to full ownership
> later if they want. We handle the migration and documentation for a one-time
> fee, once invoices are current."

### What happens if I stop paying monthly?
> "The original build and handoff are separate from ongoing support. If the
> monthly plan stops, we pause updates, monitoring, troubleshooting, and managed
> hosting. Your data and paid work aren't held hostage — if you want to export or
> transfer the system, we help with that once invoices are current."

### What do I get at the end?
> "A working system, a short walkthrough video, and simple written instructions.
> You'll know exactly how to use it, and you'll have one point of contact for
> anything you want changed."

### What do you need from me?
> "Usually your business info and services, a logo or photos if you have them,
> your current website or domain details, and a clear picture of what happens when
> a lead contacts you. If we ever need access to a tool, we prefer a collaborator
> invite instead of passwords."

### Do you need my passwords?
> "Ideally never. When we need access to something, we ask you to add us as a
> collaborator or admin — that way you never share a password and you can remove
> our access anytime. It's safer for you."

### Is my customer data safe?
> "We treat your customer data carefully and only collect what the system actually
> needs. We don't sell it, we don't scatter it around, and we follow your wishes
> on it — including deleting it if you ask."

### Can AI email or text my customers?
> "It can, but we usually start safer: AI drafts the message, a human approves it
> before it sends. You get the speed without risking a weird or wrong message
> going straight to a customer. We only turn on fully automatic sending if you
> specifically want it and understand it."

### What if AI says something wrong?
> "That's exactly why we keep a human in the loop by default — AI drafts, you or
> your team approve. AI is great at speed and organizing, but it's not perfect, so
> we build it so you stay in control of what actually goes out."

### How much does it cost?
> "It depends on the scope — a simple website is different from a full dashboard or
> automation system. The first step is a short intake, then I'll recommend the
> simplest useful starting system with a clear, specific price. No surprises."

### Why is there a monthly fee?
> "The monthly fee is optional and only for ongoing service — hosting, keeping it
> running, monitoring, and small changes. It's so you never have to think about
> the technical upkeep. If you don't want ongoing support, you don't have to pay
> it."

### What is included in monthly support?
> "Hosting and uptime, monitoring, small fixes and tweaks, and priority answers to
> your questions — plus a light check-in on what to improve next."

### What is not included?
> "Brand-new features, extra pages, or whole new systems — those are separate
> projects we'd quote clearly. Monthly support is for keeping what we built running
> smoothly, not building new things for free."

### How long does it take?
> "Most starter builds take somewhere from a few days to a few weeks, depending on
> scope. I'll give you a specific timeline with the proposal, and it assumes we get
> your info and feedback along the way."

### Can you guarantee more sales?
> "No honest person should guarantee revenue from a system alone — too much depends
> on your market and your follow-through. What we can do is improve how leads get
> captured, organized, and followed up with. That gives your business a better
> process and fewer missed opportunities."

### What if I already have a website?
> "Great — we can often work with what you have, improve it, or add the pieces
> you're missing like a lead tracker or follow-up system. I'll take a look and tell
> you honestly whether to build on it or start fresh."

### What if I use Wix, Squarespace, Shopify, GoDaddy, Google Sheets, or another tool?
> "That's completely fine — a lot of clients already use those. We either work
> alongside them or connect them so everything talks to each other. I won't make
> you rip out tools that are working; I'll tell you where they fit and where a
> small upgrade helps."

### What if something breaks?
> "If you're on a support plan, you message us and we fix it — that's what it's
> for. If you're not, we still offer support, just on a per-fix or hourly basis.
> Either way you're not left stranded."

### How do revisions work?
> "Each thing we build includes a couple of rounds of revisions to get it right.
> That's refining the agreed work. If you decide to change direction or add new
> things, that's easy too — we just quote it as a small add-on so it's clear."

### What if I do not know what I need?
> "That's completely normal, and honestly it's my job to figure out — not yours.
> We do a short intake and a quick conversation, and I'll tell you the one thing
> I'd fix first. If it's helpful, we keep going. If not, no harm done."

### Why should I hire you instead of doing it myself with ChatGPT?
> "ChatGPT is a great tool — we use AI too. But a tool isn't a system. We figure
> out what you actually need, build the pieces so they work together, keep your
> data organized, and make sure it keeps running. You get the result without
> spending your nights learning tools and stitching it together yourself."

### Why should I hire you instead of a traditional web designer?
> "A lot of web designers hand you a nice-looking site and walk away. We focus on
> the whole system — the site, capturing the lead, organizing it, and following up
> — and we keep it running for you. It's less 'pretty brochure' and more 'stop
> losing customers.'"

### What makes Laine Industries different?
> "We keep it simple and useful, we explain everything in plain English, we handle
> the technical side so you don't have to, and we're honest about what will and
> won't help. We build the smallest thing that solves your real problem first —
> not the biggest invoice."

## Bad answer vs. better answer

**"Do I need to manage the tech?"**
- ❌ Bad: "We'll use Vercel, GitHub, Supabase, n8n, environment variables, and a
  webhook."
- ✅ Better: "No. We set up the technical side so your website, form, and lead
  tracking all work together. You get the live link and a simple walkthrough — you
  don't manage the developer tools unless you want to."

**"How much does it cost?"**
- ❌ Bad: "Uhh, I'm not sure, it could be a lot of different numbers."
- ✅ Better: "It depends on scope. The first step is a short intake, then I'll
  recommend the simplest useful system with a clear price."

**"Do I own it?"**
- ❌ Bad: "You'll own everything!" *(while you plan to run it managed)*
- ✅ Better: "You own your business info, content, brand, and customers. The
  technical setup we can manage, build in your accounts, or transfer later —
  whatever fits you best."

**"Can you guarantee more sales?"**
- ❌ Bad: "Yeah, this'll definitely get you more customers."
- ✅ Better: "I won't promise revenue — nobody honest should. I can promise a
  better process for capturing and following up on leads."

**"Can AI talk to my customers?"**
- ❌ Bad: "Yep, the AI just handles all your customer messages automatically."
- ✅ Better: "We start safer — AI drafts, a human approves — so you get the speed
  without risking a wrong message going out."

## Phrases to avoid

- "GitHub / Vercel / Supabase / n8n / webhook / environment variable / deploy"
  (in front of clients).
- "I don't know, let me ask ChatGPT."
- "It'll definitely get you more sales / customers / revenue."
- "You'll own everything" (when you mean managed hosting).
- "Unlimited support" / "we'll handle anything, anytime."
- "It's easy, don't worry about it" (dismissive — explain instead).
- Anything that promises perfection, guarantees, or 24/7 availability.

## When you do not know the answer yet

It's fine not to know. It's not fine to guess on access, hosting, pricing, or
anything that affects the client's data or money. Use a "collect facts → check →
recommend" move instead of guessing.

Template:
> "That depends on your setup. I don't want to guess on [access / hosting /
> pricing] before seeing what you already use. The safe answer is we can either
> manage the technical setup for you or build inside your accounts if you prefer
> ownership. After intake, I'll recommend the cleanest option."

That answer is *more* professional than a confident guess — because guessing wrong
on access or hosting is how people lose data and trust. Behind the scenes you can
research it, ask a peer, or check your own docs, then come back with a clear
recommendation. That's consulting, not faking.

Never do:
- Guess a price you can't stand behind.
- Promise a tool can do something you haven't verified.
- Invent a technical detail to sound smart.

Always do:
- Narrow it down ("it's usually one of two paths…").
- Give the safe default.
- Commit to a specific follow-up ("I'll confirm and get back to you by [day]").

## How to say "I need to check" without sounding unprepared

Frame it as diligence, not uncertainty. You're being careful *for them*.

- > "Good question — I want to give you the right answer, not a guess. Let me
  > confirm one thing about your setup and I'll have a clear answer for you by
  > [day]."
- > "That's exactly the kind of thing I like to verify before committing, so we
  > don't run into surprises later. I'll check and follow up."
- > "It depends on a detail I'd rather confirm than assume. Give me until [day] and
  > I'll come back with a specific recommendation."
- > "I could guess, but I'd rather be right — especially on anything touching your
  > data or accounts. Let me confirm and circle back."

Then **actually follow up when you said you would.** Doing what you said, on time,
is the single biggest trust-builder in the whole sales process — and it models the
exact reliability you're selling.
