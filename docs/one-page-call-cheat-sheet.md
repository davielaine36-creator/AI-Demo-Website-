# Lane Industries — One-Page Call Cheat Sheet

Quick glance before a discovery call or client chat. Say it calm and simple.
Full version: `client-question-answer-bank.md`.

> Core move: **translate, don't explain the tech.** You get the working system;
> we handle the plumbing.

---

## 60-second pitch
> "We help small businesses stop losing leads and time. A lot of owners have
> customer info scattered across texts, calls, and memory, and follow-up slips
> when they're busy. We build simple systems that fix that — a clean website, one
> organized place for your leads, and follow-ups that get drafted for you. We
> handle the technical side, so you just get a system that works and a simple
> walkthrough. We start with the smallest useful version, so you never pay for
> stuff you don't need."

## Managed build, in one line
> "You get the working system. We handle the technical plumbing."

---

## Top 10 questions → short answers

**1. What exactly do you do?**
> "We build simple systems that help you capture leads, stay organized, and follow
> up faster — websites, lead trackers, AI-assisted follow-up. We handle the tech."

**2. How does this work?**
> "Short intake to understand your business → I recommend the simplest useful
> system with a clear price → we build and test it → we hand it off with a
> walkthrough."

**3. Where does the website/app live?**
> "On reliable hosting we set up and manage for you. You get a live link that
> works — you don't have to touch the hosting side."

**4. Do I need technical accounts?**
> "No. Developer tools and hosting are our world, not yours. We manage the
> behind-the-scenes tools unless you specifically want to own them."

**5. Do I own it?**
> "You always own your business info, content, brand, and customers. The technical
> setup we can manage for you, build in your accounts, or transfer later."

**6. What happens if I stop paying monthly?**
> "The build and handoff are separate from support. If monthly stops, we pause
> updates, monitoring, and hosting — but your data and paid work aren't held
> hostage. We help you export or transfer once invoices are current."

**7. What do you need from me?**
> "Your business info and services, a logo/photos if you have them, your current
> site or domain details, and what happens today when a lead reaches out. If we
> need access, we prefer an invite over a password."

**8. Do you need my passwords?**
> "Ideally never. We ask you to add us as a collaborator, so you never share a
> password and can remove our access anytime. Safer for you."

**9. Can AI talk to my customers?**
> "We start safer: AI drafts, a human approves before it sends. You get the speed
> without a wrong message going straight to a customer."

**10. How much does it cost?**
> "Depends on scope — a simple site is different from a dashboard or automation.
> Start with a short intake, then I'll give you a clear price for the simplest
> useful system. No surprises."

## Say often ✅
- "We keep it simple."
- "You don't need to manage the technical tools unless you want to."
- "We recommend the simplest useful system first."
- "AI drafts, a human approves."
- "I don't want to guess before seeing your setup."
- "After intake, we'll recommend the cleanest path."

## Avoid ❌
- "AI can do everything."
- "Unlimited support."
- "Guaranteed sales."
- "Just send me your password."
- "It's easy, don't worry about it."
- "I can build anything."

---

## When you don't know yet (stay calm, sound diligent)
1. > "Good question — I'd rather give you the right answer than a guess. Let me
   > confirm one thing about your setup and I'll have it for you by [day]."
2. > "That depends on your setup, and I don't guess on access or hosting. The safe
   > answer is we can manage it or build in your accounts — after intake I'll
   > recommend the cleanest option."
3. > "That's exactly the kind of thing I like to verify before committing, so we
   > avoid surprises later. I'll check and follow up by [day]."

*(Then actually follow up when you said you would — that's the trust-builder.)*

---

## Call close
> "Here's what I'd suggest: [one starter system], because it fixes the [their
> words] problem first without overbuilding. The next step is a quick intake form
> so I can put together a simple plan and a clear price. Want me to send you the
> link? I'll follow up [day] with the plan."

Always leave with a **sent link or a scheduled next step** — never a vague "let me
think about it."
