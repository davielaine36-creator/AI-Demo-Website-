## 1. How to choose the right starting system

The rule of thumb: **start with the smallest system that fixes your most painful
problem first.** Don't build everything at once.

Match the problem to the starting point:

| If this is the problem… | Start with… |
|---|---|
| "People can't clearly understand what I do, or can't easily reach me." | **Website / landing page** |
| "I have a site, but leads still don't come through cleanly." | **Website + contact / intake form** |
| "Leads get lost in texts, calls, emails, and notebooks." | **Intake + lead tracking dashboard (CRM)** |
| "I forget to follow up, or hate rewriting the same messages." | **AI-assisted follow-up drafts** |
| "I can't see what's happening in my pipeline or what needs attention." | **Small business dashboard (CRM)** |
| "New leads or jobs slip because nobody's alerted in time." | **Alerts & reminders** (usually part of a dashboard) |
| "My online store is messy — products, customer issues, day-to-day." | **Shopify / store operations support** |
| "I want someone to just handle the technical side." | **Lane Managed Build + monthly support** |
| "I honestly don't know what I need." | **Short intake → Automation Audit** |

**The starting systems, in plain terms:**

- **Website / landing page** — a clean, mobile-friendly site that explains what
  you do and gives people an obvious way to reach out.
- **Website + contact / intake form** — the above, plus a quote/contact form that
  lands somewhere organized instead of a buried inbox.
- **Intake + lead tracking dashboard (CRM / Lead Tracker)** — one place for names,
  phone numbers, job details, notes, statuses, and follow-ups.
- **AI follow-up assistant** — drafts follow-up messages based on where the
  customer is, so you never start from a blank page. **You approve before anything
  sends.**
- **Small business dashboard** — the owner's one screen: what's new, what's due,
  what needs attention.
- **Alerts & reminders** — know the moment a lead comes in or a job needs a next
  step.
- **Shopify / store operations support** — tidy products, track customer issues,
  and simple operational dashboards for an online store.
- **Automation support** — connecting the pieces (forms, alerts, follow-up
  drafting) so routine steps happen without manual effort. Usually added onto a
  build, not a first purchase.
- **Monthly managed support** — we host it, keep it running, and handle small
  changes so you never think about the technical side.

If in doubt, the honest answer is: **start with the short intake or an Automation
Audit.** We map how the business runs today and recommend the single
smallest-useful first build.

## 4. What's included / not included

**Included in a build:**

- The agreed pages / features listed in the proposal's scope.
- Basic setup and configuration.
- Testing.
- A clear handoff.
- A short walkthrough (Loom + simple written instructions).
- Up to **two rounds of revisions** per deliverable (refining the agreed work).
- Support through handoff plus a short settling-in window (about 7–14 days) for
  fixes to what we delivered.
- Clear next steps.

**Not included unless it's specifically scoped:**

- Unlimited revisions or endless changes.
- Guaranteed sales, revenue, lead counts, or business outcomes.
- Legal, tax, or compliance advice.
- Paid ads or ad management.
- Unlimited or 24/7 on-call support.
- Complex custom software beyond the agreed scope.
- **Automatic AI messages to customers without owner approval.**
- Work before a deposit is paid.
- Third-party tool subscription costs, unless we've said they're included.

New requests aren't a problem — they're just a small add-on or a "phase 2,"
quoted separately. Nothing gets built silently for free.

## 5. Ownership, hosting, and support

There are three ways to run the technical side. **Lane Managed Build is the
default.**

- **Lane Managed Build (default)** — we build it *and* run it on our
  infrastructure. You get a live system, a walkthrough, and one point of contact
  for changes. You pay a one-time build fee and (optionally) a monthly
  hosting/support fee. Best for owners who "just want it to work."
- **Client-Owned Build** — we build it on *your* accounts from the start, and hand
  over full control. Usually a higher build fee (setup + documentation), and you
  cover your own tool costs. Best if you have technical help or want to own
  everything from day one.
- **Transfer Later** — start managed, then transfer ownership to you if and when
  you ask (a one-time transfer/migration fee covers the real work of moving
  everything and documenting it). Best if you want simplicity now but the option
  to own it later.

**What you always own:** your business content and your customer relationships.
**Technical ownership** (code, hosting, accounts) depends on which model is written
into your proposal.

**If monthly support stops:** the monthly fee covers hosting, support, and
maintenance — if it stops, active hosting/support stops too. The typical sequence
is a reminder → a short grace period → the hosted system is paused → after a
longer window it may go offline. Your **data and paid-for work are never held
hostage**: once invoices are paid in full, we provide an export or arrange a
transfer (a transfer fee may apply).

## 6. Legal / trust boundaries (plain English, not legal advice)

How we operate so everyone stays protected. **This is not legal advice, and final
terms are confirmed in the written proposal or agreement.**

- **We never ask you to text or email passwords.** We use collaborator invites and
  the least access needed, for the least time. You can remove our access anytime.
- **Don't send API keys, secret keys, or sensitive login details in normal
  messages.** We use secure methods for anything sensitive.
- **Don't send payment card details or sensitive customer data over chat.** We
  collect only the data a system actually needs and handle it carefully.
- **AI drafts, a human approves.** No AI system messages your customers
  automatically unless you've specifically approved that in writing for a
  well-understood workflow.
- **No guaranteed business outcomes.** We're honest about what a system can and
  can't reasonably do; we don't promise revenue, sales, or lead numbers.
- **We honor reasonable data wishes,** including deletion requests, mirroring the
  site's privacy note.
- **For anything formal or high-value, get it in writing** — and for actual legal
  documents, an attorney's review is recommended. We provide plain working
  agreements, not legal counsel.

## 7. Client readiness checklist

To get started smoothly, it helps to have these ready (whatever you have is fine —
we can work with rough answers):

- **Business name and contact info** — how customers reach you, and where alerts
  should go.
- **Your services / offers** — what you sell and to whom.
- **Logo and photos** — if you have them (not required to start).
- **Current website / domain** — the address and platform, if any (Wix,
  Squarespace, Shopify, WordPress, or none).
- **Your current lead process** — what happens today when a lead comes in.
- **Examples** — of the messages, forms, or workflows you use now.
- **Who should receive alerts** — the person(s) who acts on new leads.
- **What success looks like** — the one outcome that would make this worth it.

Having a single main point of contact for decisions keeps everything moving.

## 8. Industry examples

Simple starting points by business type. Each includes the likely problem, a
recommended first system, and what to **avoid overbuilding** early.

### Contractor / home-service business (e.g. fencing, decks, repairs)
- **Likely problem:** quote requests arrive by call, text, and email; details
  scatter; follow-ups are inconsistent and some leads never hear back.
- **Recommended first system:** lead capture + CRM dashboard with new-lead alerts;
  add AI follow-up drafts once leads are landing in one place.
- **Avoid overbuilding:** don't start with complex scheduling or invoicing
  integrations — capture and follow up on leads first.

### Training / education (e.g. a certification or training school)
- **Likely problem:** course info, student inquiries, and renewal/requalification
  reminders live in different places, so questions and renewals slip.
- **Recommended first system:** a clear website for course info + an inquiry
  intake, then follow-up reminders for renewals.
- **Avoid overbuilding:** don't build a full learning-management platform up front
  — start with clear info and captured inquiries.

### Cleaning / window service
- **Likely problem:** lots of small quote requests; hard to respond fast enough
  and to re-engage past customers.
- **Recommended first system:** intake form + lead tracker with fast alerts; add
  follow-up drafting for past-customer re-engagement.
- **Avoid overbuilding:** don't over-automate messaging early — keep owner
  approval on follow-ups.

### Shopify / ecommerce store
- **Likely problem:** day-to-day store operations are messy — products, customer
  issues, and repetitive tasks pile up.
- **Recommended first system:** Shopify / store operations support — product
  organization, customer-issue tracking, and a simple operations dashboard.
- **Avoid overbuilding:** don't chase complex custom app development before the
  basic operations are tidy.

### Solo service provider (one-person business)
- **Likely problem:** everything lives in the owner's head and phone; leads and
  follow-ups depend on memory.
- **Recommended first system:** a simple website + contact form, or a light lead
  tracker — whichever pain is bigger.
- **Avoid overbuilding:** keep it to one screen and one workflow; a solo owner
  needs simple, not a full CRM suite.
