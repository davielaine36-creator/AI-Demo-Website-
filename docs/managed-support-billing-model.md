# Laine Industries — Managed Support & Billing Model

How we charge for building and for keeping systems running. The model is simple: a
one-time fee to build it, and an optional monthly fee to host, support, and improve
it. Ranges are starting points, confirmed per client — never guarantees.

> One-line principle: **build fee pays for the build; monthly fee keeps it alive.**
> They are separate, and the client should always know which is which.

---

## Build fee vs. monthly managed fee

- **Build fee (one-time):** covers designing and building the system, testing, and
  handoff/walkthrough. Paid as deposit + balance (see `proposal-template.md`).
- **Monthly managed fee (recurring):** covers hosting, keeping it running, support,
  and small ongoing improvements. Optional, month-to-month, cancel anytime.

A client can pay only the build fee (and self-manage or go Client-Owned), but the
**default** is Managed Build with a monthly fee.

---

## What monthly support covers

- Hosting and keeping the system online.
- Monitoring and small bug fixes to what we built.
- Minor tweaks and content/label changes (within reason).
- Priority responses to questions on the agreed channel.
- A light periodic check-in on what's working and what to improve next.

## What monthly support does NOT cover

- Brand-new features, pages, dashboards, or automations (that's a new project).
- Major redesigns or changes in direction.
- Third-party tool subscription costs (pass-through — see below).
- Unlimited same-day/emergency work unless a higher tier says so.
- Guaranteed uptime, revenue, or results.

When a request is bigger than "a small tweak," it's quoted as an add-on or phase 2.

---

## Pass-through tool costs

- Some tools cost money to run (hosting upgrades, database tiers, email/SMS
  sending, domains, n8n hosting, etc.).
- Small/standard costs are usually included in the monthly fee; **larger or
  usage-based costs are passed through** to the client, clearly and in advance.
- Never surprise a client with a tool bill — flag it before it's incurred.

---

## When to charge extra

- New functionality outside the original scope.
- A second/third system for the same client.
- Large content migrations or data cleanups.
- Rush work or off-hours emergencies (if outside their tier).
- Transfer/migration to client ownership (one-time transfer fee).

## Example support tiers

Rough monthly ranges — adjust per client and scope. Present the tier that matches
what you actually built.

| Tier | For | Roughly covers | Rough monthly |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Basic Website Care** | Simple sites/landing pages | Hosting, uptime, small text/image tweaks, security updates | ~$50–$150/mo |
| **Website + Forms Support** | Sites with quote/contact/intake forms | Basic Website Care + form monitoring, delivery checks, minor form changes | ~$100–$250/mo |
| **CRM / Dashboard Support** | Lead/CRM dashboards | Dashboard uptime, small field/status changes, data help, user support | ~$150–$400/mo |
| **Automation Support** | n8n / follow-up / alert automations | Keeping workflows running, monitoring, small logic tweaks, draft-template updates | ~$200–$500/mo |
| **Higher-touch Managed System** | Multiple connected systems | Everything above across the stack, priority response, regular improvements | ~$400–$1,000+/mo |

Tiers can combine (e.g. a site + dashboard + automations client sits in the
higher-touch range). Keep the smallest tier that genuinely fits.

## Client-friendly wording

> "There's a one-time fee to build your system. After that, there's an optional
> monthly plan where we host it, keep it running, and handle small changes and
> support — so you never have to think about the technical side. You can start,
> pause, or stop the monthly plan whenever you like. Anything brand-new we'd just
> quote separately so there are no surprises."

Avoid tech words. Say "host it and keep it running," not "Vercel/Supabase/n8n."

---

## Pause / suspend rules for nonpayment

Be clear about this before signing, so it's never a surprise:

1. **Reminder** on/near the due date.
2. **Grace period** (e.g. 7–14 days) with a friendly heads-up.
3. **Pause/suspend** the hosted system if still unpaid after grace.
4. **Longer window** (e.g. 30 days) before the system may go fully offline.
5. Restore promptly once payment is current (a small reactivation fee may apply).

Data and paid work are never held hostage — see transfer/export below.

---

## Transfer / export option

- Once **all invoices are paid in full**, the client may request an **export or
  transfer** of what they've paid for (code, data, domain, accounts).
- Transfer/migration is a **one-time fee** (it's real work: moving repos, projects,
  data, and DNS, plus documentation).
- After transfer, ongoing hosting/support becomes theirs (or a new arrangement).
- We don't withhold a client's paid-for work or data — that protects our
  reputation and referrals.

---

## Quick summary

- Build fee = one-time, builds the system.
- Monthly fee = optional, keeps it hosted/supported/improving.
- Pass through big/usage-based tool costs, flagged in advance.
- New scope = new quote.
- Nonpayment = reminder → grace → pause → offline, with data recoverable once paid.
- Ownership transfer available after full payment, for a one-time fee.
