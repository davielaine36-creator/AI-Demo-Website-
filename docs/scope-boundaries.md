# Laine Industries — Scope Boundaries

Plain-English rules that keep client work clean and protect Laine Industries. These
aren't about being rigid — they're about being clear, so projects stay friendly
and finish on time. Share the relevant parts with clients up front; it builds
trust and prevents awkward conversations later.

> One-line principle: **we deliver what's in the proposal. Anything new is a
> welcome next phase, quoted separately.**

---

## What counts as included

- Everything listed in the proposal's Scope and Deliverables.
- Building, testing, and a clear handoff of exactly those items.
- Reasonable back-and-forth to get the agreed items right.

## What counts as extra (quoted separately)

- Anything not written in the Scope/Deliverables.
- New pages, features, integrations, or automations added mid-project.
- "While you're at it…" requests — we're happy to do them as a small add-on.
- Redesigns or direction changes after work is approved and underway.

Extra work isn't a problem — it's just a new small quote or a phase 2. Nothing
gets built silently for free.

## Revision limits

- Each deliverable includes up to **two rounds of revisions**.
- Revisions mean refining the agreed work — not changing the goal or adding scope.
- Extra revision rounds are fine and billed at a simple hourly or flat add-on.

## Response-time expectations

- We aim to reply within **1 business day** on the agreed channel.
- We give clear updates at each milestone — you won't be left guessing.
- We're not on-call 24/7; urgent same-day turnarounds are arranged in advance.

## Client delay rules

- Timelines assume feedback and needed info arrive within **2–3 business days**.
- If we're waiting on the client, the delivery date moves by the delay.
- If a project stalls on the client's side for **2+ weeks**, we may pause it and
  pick back up when they're ready (deposit still applies).

## Third-party platform limits

- Some things depend on tools we don't control (Shopify, hosting, email
  providers, domain registrars, n8n, etc.).
- We work within what those platforms allow; their bugs, outages, pricing, and
  rules are outside our control.
- If a platform can't do something the client wants, we'll say so honestly and
  suggest the closest practical option.

## AI limitations (be honest)

- AI is great at drafting messages and organizing information fast.
- AI is **not** perfect — drafts should be reviewed, especially early on.
- We build AI to **support the owner's judgment**, not replace it. Owners stay in
  control of what actually sends.
- We don't promise AI will handle everything or be error-free.

## No guaranteed revenue or results claims

- We build useful systems; we **do not guarantee** revenue, lead counts, sales,
  or specific business outcomes.
- We'll always be honest about what a system can and can't reasonably do.

## No unlimited support

- A build includes support through handoff plus a short settling-in window
  ([e.g. 7–14 days] for fixes to what we delivered).
- Ongoing help is available via a simple **Monthly Support** plan — not unlimited
  free support forever.

## No work without a deposit

- Builds start **after** the deposit clears. No exceptions for new clients.
- This protects everyone's time and keeps the relationship professional.

---

*These are working boundaries, not legal terms. Keep the tone friendly — most
clients appreciate knowing exactly how things work.*
